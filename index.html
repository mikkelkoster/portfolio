The Platform

Maersk Supply Chain — the operational backbone for the world's largest container shipping network. 350+ stakeholders, 20+ product teams, 260 engineers.

My Role

First dedicated UX/Design Lead. I set the vision, built the team of 12 product designers, and shifted design from a Technology-centric function to a Product-centric one.

Supply Chain Overview

Centralised visibility across all active shipments — filterable by mode, status, and exception type.

Shipment List

Shipment Detail

The Big Bet

Maersk made a bet that would reshape global trade. Design was part of the wager.

In 2017, Maersk committed to becoming the world's first integrated logistics company — pivoting from moving containers to running customers' entire supply chains. The Supply Chain Platform was the digital product at the centre of that strategy.

I was brought in as the first-ever Design Lead to build that capability from scratch.

Before

Move containers from A to B

Maersk's value was in its ships and routes. Customers booked a container; Maersk delivered it. Revenue was tied to volume shipped.

After

Run the customer's entire supply chain

Maersk manages freight, customs, warehousing, and last-mile — end to end. Revenue tied to value delivered, not containers counted.

The Starting Point

Operators juggled 10+ disconnected tools to complete a single task.

Terminals, spreadsheets, internal web tools, and manual workarounds — a constellation of legacy systems that operators lived in every day, with no single product holding it together.

The Challenge

One platform, three user types, no design foundation — inside an engineering-led organisation.

Fragmented experience

Apps across separate tabs, different structures, no coherent flow. Users felt they were using 10 products, not one.

No task guidance

Users could complete tasks but had no path to follow — the interface didn't know what they were trying to do.

Information overload

Everything surfaced upfront regardless of context. No prioritisation, no filters that worked — just noise.

Workarounds as workflow

Without end-to-end ownership, users left the platform to get work done. The source of truth lived in email and Excel.

One platform, three user types

Customer Service, Shippers, Consignees — fundamentally different needs, one undifferentiated experience.

No design foundation

No design system, no research practice, no shared components. Starting from zero in a highly complex domain.

Engineering-dominated org

260 engineers, 20+ teams, no design processes. The risk wasn't irrelevance — it was becoming a bottleneck.

Highly technical domain

Logistics ops are dense and specialised. Designers had to earn credibility through domain fluency, not just craft.

Distributed team

12 designers across 6 nationalities and multiple time zones. Building a shared culture had to be intentional.

The Transformation

Design moved from order-taker to strategic partner.

The biggest shift wasn't a product or a tool — it was the role design played in the organisation. When I joined, design was downstream of engineering. When I left, design had a seat at the product strategy table.

Before

Design as delivery service

  • Receives specs from product and engineering
  • Executes UI requests with little context
  • No access to strategy, user research, or roadmap
  • Reactive — brought in when design is "needed"
  • Measured by output: screens delivered
→

After

Design as strategic partner

  • Co-creates product strategy and roadmap
  • Drives user research and insight synthesis
  • Direct input into platform vision and OKRs
  • Proactive — at the table before decisions are made
  • Measured by impact: outcomes enabled

Case study · 2022–2023

Matas

How do you transform Club Matas into Denmark's leading customer loyalty programme — along with a complete redesign of the app experience?

Role

Lead UX Designer

Scope

App redesign · Loyalty programme

Team

2 designers · 2 UX'ers

Industry

Beauty · Retail

The Brand

Matas is Denmark's leading beauty retailer — 1.5 million Club Matas members across all tiers. The loyalty programme had become purely transactional, with half of members receiving little perceived value and many unable to articulate why they were even a member.

My Role

Lead UX Designer end-to-end: concept exploration across multiple directions, prototyping and high-fidelity UI, facilitating 5 rounds of user testing, and presenting programme concepts and design decisions directly to the CEO and C-level steering committee at Matas.

The Brief

Club Matas must become the leading loyalty programme with clear value for customers, the market, and Matas.

That was the strategic goal set by the steering committee. The challenge: the existing programme was built on a simple earn-and-burn model that had stopped working. Members weren't engaged, the tiers felt arbitrary, and the app experience had fallen behind.

The project scope covered both the redesign of the loyalty programme mechanics and the full end-to-end app experience — 150 unique screens across all member journeys.

Before

Transactional earn-and-burn

Collect points at checkout, redeem for discounts. No personalisation, no emotional connection, no clear reason to care about your tier. 50% of members received little perceived value and didn't know why they were a member.

After

Relationship-focused loyalty

A programme built around frequency and personalisation. High-tier members get choice-based rewards and status. Low-tier members get clear point value. Everyone gets personalised inspiration and offers — moving Club Matas from transactional to relational.

What Research Revealed

Members were honest. The programme had fundamental problems that went beyond the app.

Research across member segments surfaced uncomfortable truths about what Club Matas had become — and what it needed to be.

"Personalised services create loyalty"

Member research · Multiple segments

"Club Matas needs to be simplified — it's too complicated to understand"

Member research · Low-frequency shoppers

"Many don't know why they are a member"

Member research · Cross-segment finding

"50% receive little value from the reward programme"

Strategic insight · Steering committee

"Major inequalities at the top and bottom of the hierarchy — the top 1% drives enormous turnover"

Strategic insight · Business analysis

"Focus on experiences, not just discounts"

Member research · High-frequency shoppers

The Challenge

One programme. Two fundamentally different member realities.

Frequency is the key loyalty driver — but it creates split needs. Designing for both ends of the spectrum, within a single coherent programme and app, was the central design challenge.

1.5 million members

Diverse needs across casual to obsessive shoppers — one programme and one app experience had to resonate across all of them

Points vs rewards

Designing two parallel loyalty mechanics — simple points for lower tiers and a choice-based reward model for high-frequency members

150 unique screens

End-to-end redesign covering shopping, loyalty mechanics, personalised inspiration, scan & go, and member profile

C-level alignment

Presenting programme concepts and design decisions directly to the CEO and the full steering committee — design had to be business-fluent

5 rounds of user testing

Iterative concept validation across multiple directions and fidelity levels — research was built into the process, not bolted on

Transactional → relational

Shifting from a discount programme to a relationship-focused experience meant changing the fundamental value proposition of membership

The Loyalty Design

Two parallel mechanisms. One coherent programme.

Frequency drives fundamentally different needs. Instead of a single mechanism that served neither group well, we designed distinct but complementary systems for each end of the spectrum.

Low-frequency members

Points — clear & simple

  • Points earned on every purchase
  • Usable on all products in the range
  • Special discounts on selected products
  • Transparent: 1 point = 1 kr in value
  • Easy to understand, easy to redeem

High-frequency members

Rewards — choice & status

  • Spend 1,000 DKK → unlock a reward
  • All purchases count toward the threshold
  • Choose from 4 reward categories
  • Better benefits the more you spend
  • Status recognition beyond points

The Work

150 unique screens. One coherent app experience.

From onboarding and loyalty dashboard to scan & go and personalised inspiration — we redesigned every member journey end-to-end.

01

Loyalty dashboard

At-a-glance view of tier, points balance, progress to next reward, and personalised offers — built for instant comprehension.

02

Reward selection

When high-tier members hit their spend threshold, they choose from 4 reward categories — giving agency and tangible perceived value.

03

Personalised inspiration

Curated articles, new products, and offers tuned to "My Brands" — shifting the app from transactional utility to daily destination.

04

Scan & Go

In-store mobile scanning woven directly into the loyalty flow — connecting physical and digital and making checkout frictionless.

05

Skin test & advice

Personalised product guidance based on skin profile — positioning Matas as a trusted advisor, not just a retailer.

06

Easy reorder

One-tap reorder of previously purchased products — reducing friction for the routine beauty shopping that high-frequency members do weekly.

The Result

An app that went from outdated to #4 in the App Store — and a programme that finally gave members a reason to care.

THE BIG BET

Europe is drowning in regulation. Formalize is building the platform to keep companies compliant.

Small and mid-sized companies face fast-changing EU regulatory demands armed with spreadsheets and manual workflows. Formalize streamlines that scattered process into one platform — the GRC suite for managing multiple regulations in one place.

Pain

EU regulatory compliance requirements are increasingly affecting small and medium-sized enterprises, not just large corporations.

Solution

Formalize offers an intuitive platform that delivers compliance confidence with a single source of truth for navigating existing, new, and future regulations.

THE STARTING POINT

Two products with real traction — built fast, and built by engineers.

When I joined, Formalize already had two products in market and growing. Both were built engineering-first — shipped fast to seize the opportunity. What the company hadn't built yet was a design function.

If you’re not investing in UX, you’re investing in luck.

The mantra I brought to Formalize.

THE CHALLENGE

Product-market fit without design fit — in a domain where trust is everything.

Design as afterthought

No full-time designers. 1–2 part-time freelancers pulled in occasionally for complex tasks.

Inconsistent platform

Customers: "Features work differently across the platform — it comes off as unprofessional."

A failed first attempt

One previous design-system effort had already failed. The second had to stick.

Highly complex domain

GRC is dense and regulatory. Designers had to build domain fluency fast.

Dense information

Enterprise B2B SaaS with heavy data and workflows — clarity is the product.

No product structure

No Product Managers, no triads. Engineering set the direction.

Built fast to win

Speed was the priority. Quality and consistency suffered for it.

Two products, one bar

Formalize and Whistleblower Software both needed the same standard of craft.

Trust is the business

A compliance platform that feels unprofessional undermines the very thing it sells.

THE APPROACH

Hands-on, research-led, and close to engineering.

The principles behind how I built design into the company — not from a distance, but inside the work.

Hands-on, not hands-off

I designed inside a product team while setting direction — close to the craft, not managing it from a distance.

Research before pixels

Decisions start with evidence. The design refresh was pitched on what customers told us and what good design returns — not on taste.

Built with engineering

Design and engineering co-created the system and components — so what we designed is what actually shipped.

THE JOURNEY

From on-demand help to design built into how Formalize solves problems and ships features.

06

A clear vision for the platform

Defined where Formalize is going — a modern, fast platform that's easy to grasp — and aligned the team behind it.
